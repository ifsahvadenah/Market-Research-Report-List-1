<p><p>The industrial rubber market consists of two primary types: natural rubber and synthetic rubber. Natural rubber is derived from latex, mainly sourced from rubber trees, and is known for its excellent elasticity and resilience, making it ideal for products like tires and seals. In contrast, synthetic rubber is chemically manufactured from petroleum byproducts, offering enhanced durability, heat resistance, and flexibility. It is widely used in automotive, construction, and footwear industries. Both types play vital roles in various applications, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
