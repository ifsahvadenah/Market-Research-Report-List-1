<p><p>Froth flotation frothers are crucial in mineral processing, enhancing the separation of valuable minerals from ores. The market is divided into two main types: surface-active agents and nonsurfactant frothers. Surface-active agents, such as alcohols and glycols, reduce surface tension, promoting bubble formation and stability in froth. Nonsurfactant frothers, on the other hand, do not alter surface tension significantly but improve froth characteristics through their physical properties. Both types play vital roles in optimizing flotation efficiency and overall recovery rates.</p></p>
