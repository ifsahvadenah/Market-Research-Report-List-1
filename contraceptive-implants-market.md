<p><p>The contraceptive implants market consists primarily of two types: Levonorgestrel and Etonogestrel contraceptive implants. Levonorgestrel implants release the hormone levonorgestrel, providing effective pregnancy prevention for up to three years. Etonogestrel implants, on the other hand, deliver etonogestrel and offer protection for up to three years as well. Both types work by inhibiting ovulation and thickening cervical mucus, thus preventing fertilization. The choice between them typically depends on individual health considerations and preferences.</p></p>
