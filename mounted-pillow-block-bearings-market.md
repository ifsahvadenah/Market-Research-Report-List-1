Ball bearings utilize spherical elements to reduce friction, ideal for high-speed applications. In contrast, roller bearings employ cylindrical rollers, offering increased load capacity and stability, making them suitable for heavy-duty environments. Both types find applications in industries such as manufacturing, automotive, and agriculture, enhancing performance and durability of mechanical systems.</p></p>
